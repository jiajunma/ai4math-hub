## Abstract

We explore the capability of large language models to translate informal mathematical statements into formal proof assistant code. This task, called autoformalization, has been a long-standing goal in formal methods.

## Key Findings

- GPT-4 can autoformalize ~25% of competition math problems
- Self-consistency filtering improves results
- Formalization quality correlates with informal understanding

## Significance

Demonstrates the potential for LLMs to bridge informal and formal mathematics.
