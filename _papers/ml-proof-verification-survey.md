## Abstract

We survey the landscape of machine learning techniques applied to mathematical proof verification. This includes neural network-based approaches, symbolic methods, and hybrid systems.

## Coverage

- **Neural methods**: Embeddings, transformers, graph networks
- **Symbolic methods**: Automated theorem provers, SMT solvers
- **Hybrid approaches**: Combining neural and symbolic
- **Evaluation**: Benchmarks and metrics

## Key Findings

- Hybrid approaches show most promise
- Data scarcity remains a challenge
- Domain adaptation is crucial
- Evaluation needs standardization

## Future Directions

- Better integration of neural and symbolic
- Automated data generation
- Cross-domain transfer
- Human-AI collaboration
